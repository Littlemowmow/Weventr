Short answer: **yes, it matters.** When someone clicks from a DM, the **waitlist form should be visible immediately** without scrolling.

In growth terms this is called **reducing friction in the conversion funnel**. Every extra step (scrolling, reading, searching) reduces sign-ups.

### Best practice

When someone clicks your link from that message, they should land on **one of these two things instantly**:

1. **The waitlist form above the fold**
2. **Directly scrolled to the waitlist section**

---

# Recommended Fix (Very Easy)

Use a **section anchor link**.

Example:

```
sidequest.com/#waitlist
```

Then on your site the form section has:

```
id="waitlist"
```

So when people click the link they land **right at the form**.

You still keep the rest of the page below for people who want to explore.

---

# Why This Matters

When sending DMs like your message:

Hey! I’m building a startup at the University of Michigan called **SideQuest**.

We just submitted our **TestFlight beta to Apple**, and while we wait for approval we’re letting people join the early access list.

If you want to try it when the beta opens, you can join the waitlist here:

[link]

people are usually:

* on their phone
* clicking quickly
* not reading the page carefully

If they click and **don’t see a form immediately**, many will leave.

Even small friction drops conversions.

Typical effects:

| Landing Experience     | Conversion |
| ---------------------- | ---------- |
| Form visible instantly | 25–40%     |
| Requires scrolling     | 10–20%     |
